Bullet Journal Spreads

PURPOSE

A bullet journal is an free-form method of journaling that allows people to customize they way information is organize and sorted. Instead of the traditional lines in a notebook, a bullet journal has dots in grid form, allowing for flexibility in formatting. Most people toy with the idea of getting a bullet journal but are intimidated to start because they don’t know what to put on the page or want to be artsy with it.

I digitally recreated my minimalistic bullet journal spreads to share with friends if they are ever stuck at the “I don’t know where to start” stage.

DEVELOPMENT

I modeled the blank bulleted page off my current Leuchtturm bullet journal notebook because it is the standard size for bullet journaling. I measured the distance between each bullet and applied it to my designs.

I then created spreads that I personally use the most.

  1. “Grid Spacing” - beneficial to know how many dots you have in each page, across and down. This also shows you how many rows or columns you can place based on a certain, equal width. Each journal is different, so create your grid with that in mind.

  2. “Tickets, please” - I love going to concerts and other events, so I have a spread documenting where I went and when. I made variations on each ticket, so you can pick the design you like to implement in your own journal.

  3. “Habit Tracker” - I developed a simple, 1-page system of keeping track of all my habits in a month. Write down the habits you want to track at the bottom, one for each column, and track per day via each row.

  4. “An Attitude of Gratitude” - I keep a gratitude journal to help me stay positive, so I wanted to share my minimalist approach! This spans across 2 pages. Each page holds up to 16 boxes, 1 for each day of the month. In each box, I write out what I was grateful for. There will be an extra spot or 2, so fill it up with an illustration or quote of your own.

  5. “Weekly Spread” - after years of trying out various bullet journal spreads, this is the one that I stuck with. To me, 1 or 2 pages per 7 days takes up too much space because I don’t have much to do per day. Instead, I fit 8 days in 1 page. If you prefer to keep 1 week on 1 page, feel free to spread it out! I keep 8 because I like the balance. I don’t like giving one day more space for things than another.

I used Figma to design the bullet journal spreads because the link to the project is easily sharable. And, if anyone is a Figma user , they would be able to view the dimensions themselves.
