Coloring Book

PURPOSE

As a side project, I wanted to combine the excitement of listening to your favorite song with calming activity of coloring. I transformed cover albums of popular songs into black and white printables, available for anyone to begin their interpretation.

PROCESS

First, I sent out a survey to my friends asking what their most-listened to genre of music was over the last year, which was electronic. Afterward, I researched top-played songs and recent releases on Youtube. I compiled a playlist of over 100 songs on Spotify, then narrowed down the list to remove any repeated artists and styles of music. I chose to start working on four songs. I found images of the album art for each song, off of which I’d base my work.

The first image I wanted to transform was a beautiful, multi tiered flower. I wanted to utilize Photoshop to remove all colors and darken the outline for me. While this was a good idea in theory, it left me a greyscaled image that would take away from a fun coloring user experience.

Previous Creative

Photoshop -> Greyscale

I turned to Figma to use an Image Tracer plugin, similar to the Image Tracer tool on Illustrator. I used this function for both the original and the greyscaled to compare end products, but the because of the heavy gradient usage, the traced image of the original had so many vector lines, it was almost completely black.

Greyscale image, traced. Some gaps

Instead, I worked with the traced greyscaled image, which was closer to my final vision. There were some areas that were open-ended because the Image Tracer tool could not pick up the edges.

End result

To finish, I used the pen tool to fill in the gaps.

Because the rest of my images weren’t as detailed or dense as this flower, I decided to mainly use the pen tool for the remainder of the project.

OTHER IMAGES

RESULT

I presented the pages with the context that these images would remind them of some of their favorite songs. I did not show the original album art images.

The coloring pages were well received. Many users could connect the outlines to the original image. People were reminded of the fun times they had at concerts that played these songs. I deemed this project a success!
