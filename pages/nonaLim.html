Visual Design

PURPOSE

I was a Visual Designer at Nona Lim, a company that produces packaged Asian comfort food, During my time there, I designed food infographics, customized their Shopify website using HTML + CSS, and edited their recipe videos with Adobe Premiere Pro. Additionally, I helped define the brand identity, system, and creative direction of web and social media marketing material.

PROCESS

DESIGN

I made summer-themed infographics using Adobe Illustrator. The first was to highlight recipes that features Nona Lim’s delicious, packaged noodles. The second was an infographic that was to be shown at San Francisco’s Pride Festival at Nona Lim’s stall serving Cold Noodle Salad. We had 2 iterations; In the first, we show a bright rainbow background, but the handdrawn food was washed out. In the second, we faded out the background so the food was more easily seen. We also replaced the original food icons with vector images to provide a uniform look. Additonally, I provided templates for ads to be shown on Facebook.

VIDEO

Nona Lim wanted to create video recipes for her customers to follow along, so I drew a storyboard that showed the order of ingredients to use as well as different video perspectives.

WEB

I updated and customized Nona Lim’s website though Shopify. Before, the website appeared clunky and not food=focused. In the new version, we wanted to highlight Nona Lim’s food in all its delicious glory!

RESULT

All in all, I had a great time during my summer internship at Nona Lim. I got to experience the behind the scenes of running a food business, a topic close to my heart. I’m grateful for Nona Lim giving me the opportunity to develop my visual design skills for her company!
