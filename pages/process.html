Optimize Creative Processes

CHALLENGE

At Lifestreet, I strive to optimize processes to make daily tasks easier to complete, thus freeing up more time for more impactful work. One of the last projects I lead was to optimize the handoff process between Product Managers and Designers. Before, the process was repetitive, tedious, and uncollaborative. Product Managers would take over 1 day to ideate the entire flow. I wanted to assist collaboration across departments and increase efficiency.

RESEARCH

First, I conducted a survey within the team of Product Managers to pinpoint the main issue for developing a product.

Our design team had been using Sketch to translate the Product Manager’s flow from a Jira ticket into a storyboard, which then got exported into a PDF for Developers to then code the product. In addition, Designers provided a Zeplin file which specified positioning of elements within the storyboard.

I had heard of Figma from friends at other companies, and that Figma was starting to feel like the new industry standard for User Interactive design. I looked into the benefits.

COLLABORATION: No need to walk over to someone’s desk (we’re in quarantine so this is impossible anyway) - simply send the file and look at it in at the comfort of your own seat. Sketch only allowed the owner of the file to make edits. However, in Figma both the Product Manager and Designer could edit and make comments directly on the file.

VERSION CONTROL: Free version includes 30d history, where you can see who edited what, when.

PRICING: free - $45/month

I wanted everyone on an individual account to start in case there were advantages with other programs that would’ve prevented us from fully swapping over to Figma.

EASE OF INTEGRATION: It is incredibly simple to port Sketch files to Figma.

I was sold! I ran the idea by the Design team and we decided to try working on our next product solely in Figma.

DEVELOPMENT

Armed with this information, I drafted a new process for Product Manager and Designer in the creation of a product. The main goal was to remove the feeling of the PM telling the Designer what to do, but to have both the Product Manager and Designer feel creative in the process.

Like anything new, there were a couple hiccups during the Figma migration. Some main complaints lamented on the difficulty of implementing specials fonts and complications for exporting screens into a single PDF file. For these, we needed to research more plugins and learn more about how Figma functions.

SOLUTION

After the completion of our first product entirely on Figma, I asked for feedback. I received overwhemingly positive comments from Product Managers.

“It was so nice to be able to talk over the flow with someone else. I loved being able to see what was being put on the storyboard in real time. It was easy for me to make comments too.”

I believe we accomplished our goal of being transparent and increasing collaboration.

Moving forward, Designers will continue to research more techniques. I will be learning alongside them. I’m excited to keep learning in Figma!
