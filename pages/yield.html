Increased Yield in Creative

CLIENT

At Lifestreet, I plan and execute One of our enterprise accounts wanted their ad to appeal more to their demographic. The key words to describe them are “achievement masters”, “solve problems”, and “complete collections”.

CHALLENGE

The client wanted us to refresh their playable ad that would appeal to the descriptors of people above. The goal was to have at least a 20% observed increase in conversion rate (CVR).

PROCESS

I created a persona map to pinpoint which parts of the playable I wanted to keep and change.

One note was particularly interesting to me: “was so engaging, want to play more levels”. Digging deeper on this point, I wanted to give users a clearer focus on the playable’s objective to help them challenge themselves and gain mastery of the ad, resulting in a higher desire to click through and convert.

SOLUTION

The background used previously was seasonal, leading to high ad fatigue. Because of this image, the objective in the top header UI was difficult to see. I wanted to test a darker background that was not only aligned with the game’s theme, but also would make the information pop. I tested using best practices on sizing, gameplay style, and layout.

RESULT

The new creative yieled extremely strong performance across publishers and exchanges, producing a 56.62% observed lift in the impressions to install rate (ITI%).

CONCLUSION

The results show that a gameboard occupying more phone space, uses bigger icons, and shows a clear objective at the top resonates well with users.
